Background

My name is Andrew Rios and I was born October 2nd, 2004 (19 years old). I was born here in the U.S. but I have a hispanic background from Mexico and Venezuela. A big part of my cultural background is religion and the values that come with being a Christian. Being the oldest out of two siblings my biggest value and strength is determination. To be able to pass these values down to them is something I find significant and influential on our relationship.

Goals

Through out my time at the UW I want to gain as much experience in programming as I can that will help me choose the career that is right for me. After getting my degree in CS I want to further my education and start grad school. All of this is so that I can repay my parents for everything they have given my brothers and I. Knowing how much my parents have sacrifieced to give me a life in the U.S., and how much more they sacrifice everyday to keep us happy, is what pushes me the most to achieve these goals.

Work Experience

For the past year and a half I've been working as a head lifeguard at the Mount Rainier Pool. Along with 7 other head guards, I was responsible for 35 staff in a high pressure enviroment. Each month I helped conduct staff trainings and was responsible for the attendance of staff to these trainings. Lastly, I trained new staff in areas of lifeguarding, swim lessons, and customer service.

Hobbies

The two main hobbies I have currently are snowboarding and coding. I consider coding a hobby because although most of it is from studying and doing homework, I find it fun and intriguing. I get the same feelings from working on code that I do from playing video. When I'm coding, it's like time speeds up and hours have passed when it feels like its only been minutes. Another enjoyment of life that I have is snowboarding. Like coding, snowboarding is a source of dopamine for me. A way to get away from life and responsibilities. And when I'm not snowboarding or coding, I spend time with my family and friends.
